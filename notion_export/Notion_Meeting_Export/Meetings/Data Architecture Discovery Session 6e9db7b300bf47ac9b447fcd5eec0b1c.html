MES (Manufacturing Execution System)

  • Central hub connecting controls, Optiva, and M3
  • Manages batching information
  • Home-built application
  • Different MES solutions used across facilities
  • Key contact: Matt Pennaz

ANV (Acidity & Viscosity)

  • Extension of MES — lives in same database
  • Real-time tool for monitoring cooking processes
  • Tracks test results against "golden batch" curves
  • Two data points:
    • Acid number — manual entry by operator
    • Viscosity — automated from viscometer instrument
  • Recommends adjustments (maleic or glycol) to get batch back on curve
  • Also known as ONIX by some

OATS Application

  • Also in MES database
  • All tied together by batch ID (main key across all systems)

MPD SHAR

  • Big mixer system
  • Described as "MES light" for Molding Products

PCD (Process Controlled Data)

  • Interfaces with PLCs for raw material charging
  • Parameters for specific products that talk to PLCs

📦 Product Information Systems

Optiva (PLM - Product Lifecycle Management)

  • Stores formulas, specifications, revisions
  • Used by R&D and QC technicians
  • Contains raw material specs and finished goods test requirements

SDS (Safety Data Sheets)

  • Formerly MSDS (Material Safety Data Sheets)
  • Documentation for proper handling of chemical products

  • For capital expenditure planning and approvals

ETQ (Excellence Through Quality)

⚠️ Data Quality Challenges

Manual vs. Automated Entry

  • PLC-driven measurements are exact (down to 0.00)
  • Manual entries often have operators entering expected values, not actual
    • Example: Recipe calls for 20,600 lbs, they put in exactly 20,600 — "amazing accuracy"
  • Makes traceability and analysis challenging
  • Good PLC data enables bad batch investigation

System Variations

  • Three different ways of doing things across facilities
  • Need for data domain restructuring
  • Goal: "One lake" database/interface for reporting

🔗 Key Data Flows

MES → M3

  • Primarily for inventory management
  • Does NOT update costing or formulation details
  • Reduces raw material inventory based on consumption
  • Increases finished goods inventory based on production

Batch Traceability

  • Each batch has a GUID tied to specific plant formula
  • Batch records link to product/formulation IDs from M3
  • Full traceability from lowest granularity to higher hierarchies ✅
