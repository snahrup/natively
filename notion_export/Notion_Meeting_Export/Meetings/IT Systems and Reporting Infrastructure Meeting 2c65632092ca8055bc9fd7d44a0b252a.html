Summary

Meeting Overview

  • Session focused on introducing Steve to the company's reporting infrastructure and data sources [1]
  • Discussion centered on how reports are organized, data sources available, and current reporting processes [2] [3]
  • Meeting served as part of Steve's onboarding to understand the IT landscape before deeper engagement [4]

Reporting Structure

  • Reports are currently grouped by data source or function, with some organized by system [2]
  • When Molding Products and NAC were acquired, they maintained the same folder structure as Interplastic [3]
  • Future goal is to organize reports by function (e.g., AP folder) without users needing to know which company or system the report comes from [5]
  • Current structure may be revised, and newer SSRS features could enable better organization [6] [7]

Data Sources Overview

  • Comprehensive review of all available data sources for reporting [8]
  • Key systems include:
    • M3 (both cloud and on-premises versions) for ERP functions [9] [10]
    • ETQ ("Excellence Through Quality") for quality issues, safety, and agency requirements [11] [12]
    • CPR (Capital Planning Requests) for RFEs [13]
    • EAM (Enterprise Asset Management) for maintenance work orders [13]
    • Thermograph for production testing and measuring viscosity [14] [15] [16]
    • Optiva Live for formula and raw material data (R&D sandbox) [17]
    • SysAid for ticketing [18]
    • Various other specialized databases [19] [20]

System Integration

  • Data flows from M3 to ETQ (one-way integration) [21] [22]
  • On-premises M3 servers enable workflows and document management with hyperlink functionality [9]
  • Discussion about potential for more resilient linking between systems [23] [24] [25] [26]

Challenges and Considerations

  • No standardized stakeholder system for report requests [27]
  • Users sometimes repurpose reports for different functions than intended [28]
  • Need to consolidate reporting across multiple acquired companies [3]
  • Steve's main challenge will be understanding how systems interconnect and affect each other [29]
  • Some reporting may eventually be replaced by BI solutions [30]
