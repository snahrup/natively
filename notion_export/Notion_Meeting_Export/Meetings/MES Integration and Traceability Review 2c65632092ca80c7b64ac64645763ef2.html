Formulation Flow Overview

  • Optiva holds corporate formulas & specs (percent‑based, sum = 100%)
  • MES creates plant‑specific formula (Plant Formula ID) linked to corporate formula ID
  • Batch cut snapshots corporate version, plant version, spec version – immutable thereafter
  • Batch ID travels through MES → ANV/OATS → M3; M3 appends lot suffix (‑A/‑B/‑C) for stock allocation

Plant Formula IDs & Traceability

  • Two tables: CorporateFormula (global ID) and PlantFormula (own ID + foreign key to corporate)
  • Batch record stores both PlantFormulaID and CorporateFormulaID (plus versions)
  • Badge/Batch number is the system‑wide key; joins to tests, adjustments, cycle times, dispositions
  • When a batch is split, MES re‑evaluates eligible plant formulas based on vessel/group & size range

Vessel & Batch‑Size Constraints

  • Plant formulas can restrict:
    • Vessel group or specific kettle (e.g., B‑5)
    • Batch‑size bands (e.g., <10k lb, 10‑40k lb)
  • Enforcement occurs at batch creation; if vessel becomes unavailable later, batch is cancelled or re‑cut
  • Pending batches can be edited: change vessel, adjust size, add rework lines, tweak formula per‑batch

Scheduling & Rescheduling

  • Advanced Scheduler seeds batch card with formula, vessel, size, and default constraints
  • On reschedule or vessel change, MES re‑validates constraints before issuing the new batch card
  • If a kettle is down day‑of, operator must cancel and recut or select an alternate allowed vessel

Cycle‑Time & Analytics

  • Steps captured: Formulation → QC → Adjustments → Pump‑out (multiple passes possible)
  • Duration recorded either automatically from PLC events or manually entered on batch card
  • PLC entries flagged as "authoritative"; manual entries carry a flag for provenance
  • Time‑to‑correct calculated by joining adjustment timestamp to next QC result timestamp
  • Rework or multiple QC passes generate separate records (record #3, #4, …)

Data Capture: PLC vs Manual

  • PLC badge data flows directly into MES tables (high‑precision timestamps, exact quantities)
  • Manual entry used when PLC not available; flagged in underlying tables
  • Operators sometimes batch‑enter data from paper cards at day‑end – creates wide time windows

ANV & OATS Recommendations

  • ANV stores recommended adjustment (value, session ID) in MES tables (not shown in UI)
  • Operators may apply a different amount; both recommended and actual values linked to same Batch ID
  • Enables metrics: Recommended vs Actual, Operator compliance, Time‑to‑correct per plant & vessel

Disposition & Yield Accounting

  • MES sends per‑line product condition and exact weight to M3 (salable, rework, scrap)
  • Yield variance computed from target fill vs total weight on M3 side
  • By‑product handling:
    • XL‑Mix / X‑Lot – reworkable material routed back into eligible batches
    • Wash Styrene – recorded as distinct non‑salable consumption with its own lot and disposal method
  • Stub drums and over‑production tracked as separate disposition lines

Integration Failures & Exception Handling

  • Approx. 98‑99% of MES → M3 postings succeed; 1‑2% fail
  • Common failure reasons:
    • Timing mismatch (batch close arrives before raw‑material consumption)
    • Non‑sequential processing in MECH integration (raw‑material transactions sent after batch closed)
  • No acknowledgment from MECH beyond "received"; need explicit success/failure response
  • Planned cloud migration expected to reduce timing issues and enable richer API calls

Mass Balance & S3/S4 Terminology

  • IP refers to mass‑balance accountability as "S3/S4" – umbrella for yield accounting & inventory reconciliation
  • Every pound tracked to one of three outcomes: Salable, Reworkable, Scrap/Waste
  • Glossary to align executive view (Yield Accounting) with operational terms (S3/S4, X‑Lot, XL‑Mix)
