Summary

Current State Assessment

  • Company data management is described as "a disaster" with no systematic management of data in place. [1]
  • The organization has grown rapidly from a $200M business five years ago to over $1B now, with multiple divisions from historical acquisitions. [2] [3]
  • Core applications include two ERP systems (M3 on-prem and M3 cloud) and Salesforce. [4]
  • Many processes are manual and outside the systems, with plans to map processes and drive toward best practice configuration. [4] [5]
  • Financial systems are "underdefined" with poor GL structure - for example, there is no dedicated IT budget, just a G&A cost center that includes all corporate functions. [6] [7]

Data Management Challenges

  • Product naming/numbering schemes were highlighted as a significant issue requiring standardization across divisions. [8] [9] [10]
  • Current database structures lack clear naming conventions, making querying difficult. [11]
  • The company is facing a "maturation timeframe" from a data generation standpoint. [12]
  • Legacy processes and systems are in place (including petty cash and PO-driven business controls). [13] [14]
  • Data standardization, classification, and lineage tracking are all needed. [15] [16]

Governance Structure

  • Two steering committees have been established:
    • Business Applications committee led by Robin Virginia (focusing on ERP and Salesforce) [17] [18]
    • Industry 4.0 committee led by Mike (focusing on Operations, Manufacturing, R&D, Quality) [18]
  • The CIO and COO sit on both committees to ensure coordination. [19] [20]
  • Data domains and ownership/stewardship concepts were introduced to executives. [21] [22]
  • Executive leadership is supportive of the initiative with funding already secured. [23]

Technical Approach

  • Microsoft Purview will be used for data catalog and data glossary to minimize manual documentation. [24] [25]
  • Documentation needs to be actionable and accessible, not just static files. [26] [27]
  • Need for data architecture drawings showing system connectivity, master sources, and data flows. [28] [29]
  • Data lineage and traceability are important for understanding data origins and transformations. [16]
  • Manufacturing engineering group is already working with a vendor on OT data collection aligned with Microsoft Fabric. [30] [31] [32]

Stakeholder Engagement

  • Initial stakeholder presentation will be to the Executive Leadership Team (ELT), scheduled for mid-February. [33] [34]
  • Broader engagement will follow with steering committees to involve key leaders throughout the organization. [35]
  • Steve will be positioned as an internal data architect rather than as a consultant to avoid resistance. [36] [37]
  • Mike emphasized treating Steve as part of the team: "You are one of us." [38]
  • Approach will be collaborative: "I'm not going to act like I know everything about it because I don't." [39] [36]

Strategic Importance

  • Master data management is foundational to all other initiatives on the technology roadmap. [40] [41]
  • Process modernization, ERP migration, NES replacement, and AI initiatives all depend on solid data management. [42]
  • Leadership recognizes this is about "growing up" as an organization. [3]
  • Data initiative has executive support with Mike as the sponsor: "My name is tied to it." [43] [44]
